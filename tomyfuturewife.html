To my future wife by Andrew Nguyen
I can’t wait to share my dreams and aspirations with you
To make our dreams and aspirations old memories
To taste what the world has to offer with you
Let’s fly and travel
Let’s jump out of a plane
Let’s play in an amusement park we can’t pronounce the name of
I can’t wait to attend our own wedding
To have our friends and family watch us
Close one chapter and open another
I can’t wait to refer to you as my wife
I can’t wait to hold our own children
To feel a love only a parent could
To impart our knowledge to them
To raise them to be kind-hearted and compassionate
I can’t wait to grow old with you
To become grandparents
To enjoy the radiance of newborns all over again
I can’t wait to live away our days
To laugh, eat, and sleep with you
To live with only a single care in the world for each other
To smile on my last day on Earth
When I pass away, I hope you don’t cry too much
I hope you don’t forget to smile
I hope our friends and family console you well
I hope you remember that I would only want you to be happy
When I return to our house in a jar
I hope you don’t join me too quickly
But when you do, I can’t wait for our ashes to mix indifferently
So I can feel the warmth of your arms once more
But this time, I won’t let go.
But first, I can’t wait to meet you.
