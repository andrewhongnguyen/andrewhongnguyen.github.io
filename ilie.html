I Lie by Andrew Nguyen
Minty breeze,
Your children Aura and Pontus
Drift and play nearby.
Without shoes and socks
Grass crinkle under my toes and her’s.
How vast and heavenly
The sky is
Is unbelievable.
Laughing,
On the ground next to her
I lie.
After the sun walks home
The darkened world
Basks in moonlight and stars.
We embrace the chilled crisp air,
Our eyelids stumble and falls.
Beside your beauty, I wrap my arms around myself
in your bed
I lie.
However,
Man tore down your wooden bed frame
To make space for his suburban cities.
Now he pollutes the sky with smog
And the ocean with his selfishness.
On behalf of Man,
I’m sorry, Gaia.
“He’ll return your home someday”
with a smile next to your side
I lie.
